### Question 1 — Homeowners
The HO-3 provides open-peril coverage on which of the following?

A. Personal property (Coverage C)
B. Dwelling and other structures (Coverage A and B)
C. All six coverage parts
D. Only Coverage A (Dwelling)

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B.** The HO-3 is a hybrid: open peril on the dwelling (A) and other structures (B), named peril on personal property (C). This is one of the most fundamental and frequently tested concepts.
</details>

---

### Question 2 — Homeowners Sub-Limits
A client's jewelry collection is worth $12,000. Their HO-3 has standard sub-limits. Without any endorsements, what is the maximum the policy will pay for theft of the entire collection?

A. $12,000
B. $2,500
C. $1,500
D. $500

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. $1,500.** The HO-3 sub-limit for jewelry, watches, and furs is $1,500 for theft. A scheduled personal property endorsement is needed for the full value.
</details>

### Question 3 — Homeowners Coinsurance
A home has a replacement cost of $500,000. The owner insures it for $350,000. The policy has an 80% coinsurance clause. The owner suffers a $100,000 covered loss. How much does the insurer pay?

A. $100,000
B. $87,500
C. $80,000
D. $70,000

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. $87,500.** Required amount: 80% × $500K = $400K. Ratio: $350K / $400K = 0.875. Payment: 0.875 × $100K = $87,500.
</details>

### Question 4 — Personal Auto Liability
Insurance under the PAP primarily follows:

A. The driver
B. The car
C. The state of registration
D. The highest-limit policy

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. The car.** The owner's policy on the vehicle is primary. The driver's own policy is excess. Insurance follows the car first, then the driver.
</details>

### Question 5 — PAP Physical Damage
Your client hits a deer on the highway. This claim is classified as:

A. Collision
B. Other Than Collision (Comprehensive)
C. Uninsured Motorist
D. Not covered under the PAP

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. Other Than Collision.** Contact with an animal is specifically classified as OTC/comprehensive under the PAP, not collision. This matters because OTC claims often don't increase premiums and may have a different deductible.
</details>

### Question 6 — UM/UIM Stacking
In a stacking state, a client has three vehicles on their PAP with $100,000 UM per vehicle. They're hit by an uninsured driver with $250,000 in injuries. What UM coverage is available?

A. $100,000
B. $200,000
C. $250,000
D. $300,000

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**D. $300,000.** In a stacking state, UM limits are added across all vehicles: 3 × $100K = $300K. The $250K claim is fully covered.
</details>

### Question 7 — Umbrella
Which of the following is covered by a personal umbrella's drop-down coverage but typically NOT by the underlying HO policy?

A. Slip-and-fall on the insured's property
B. Fire damage to the dwelling
C. Defamation (libel/slander)
D. Windstorm damage to the roof

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Defamation.** Personal injury (libel, slander, defamation) is often excluded from the HO but covered by the umbrella. When the umbrella covers something the underlying doesn't, it "drops down" after the self-insured retention.
</details>

### Question 8 — Umbrella Underlying Requirements
A client has a $1M umbrella requiring $300K underlying auto liability. They actually carry $100K. They cause a $500K accident. How much is the client personally responsible for?

A. $0
B. $100,000
C. $200,000
D. $500,000

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. $200,000.** Auto pays $100K (actual limit). Umbrella applies above the *required* limit ($300K), so it pays $200K ($500K - $300K). The $200K gap between the actual ($100K) and required ($300K) limits is the client's responsibility.
</details>

### Question 9 — Life Insurance
Which type of life insurance builds cash value?

A. Term life
B. Whole life
C. Annual renewable term
D. Decreasing term

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. Whole life.** Term insurance (including ART and decreasing term) provides only death protection with no savings component. Permanent insurance (whole, universal, variable) builds cash value.
</details>

### Question 10 — Life Insurance Tax
Life insurance death benefits paid to a named beneficiary are generally:

A. Fully taxable as ordinary income
B. Taxable only on the gain above premiums paid
C. Income-tax-free
D. Subject to capital gains tax

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Income-tax-free.** Under IRC Section 101, life insurance death proceeds paid to a beneficiary are received free of income tax. This is one of the most significant tax advantages of life insurance.
</details>

### Question 11 — Disability Insurance
The elimination period in a disability insurance policy is best described as:

A. The maximum benefit period
B. The waiting period before benefits begin
C. The period after which pre-existing conditions are covered
D. The time to process the application

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. The waiting period before benefits begin.** The elimination period functions as a time-based deductible. Longer elimination periods reduce premiums.
</details>

### Question 12 — Health Insurance
A client has a $2,000 deductible, 80/20 coinsurance, and $8,000 out-of-pocket maximum. They incur $50,000 in medical bills. What is their total out-of-pocket cost?

A. $2,000
B. $8,000
C. $11,600
D. $10,000

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. $8,000.** Deductible: $2,000. Coinsurance on remaining $48,000: 20% = $9,600. Total would be $11,600, but the out-of-pocket maximum caps it at $8,000.
</details>

### Question 13 — Medicare
Which part of Medicare covers inpatient hospital stays?

A. Part A
B. Part B
C. Part C
D. Part D

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**A. Part A.** Part A covers inpatient/hospital, skilled nursing (limited), hospice, and some home health. Part B covers outpatient/doctor visits. Part C is Medicare Advantage. Part D is prescription drugs.
</details>

### Question 14 — Risk Assessment
According to the risk management matrix, which combination of frequency and severity is most appropriate for insurance?

A. High frequency, high severity
B. High frequency, low severity
C. Low frequency, high severity
D. Low frequency, low severity

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Low frequency, high severity.** Insurance is designed for events that are unlikely but financially devastating. High-frequency losses are better handled through avoidance or retention, and low-severity losses are best retained.
</details>

### Question 15 — Underwriting
A CLUE report tracks:

A. Credit scores and payment history
B. Driving violations and license status
C. Claims history for 7 years
D. Property tax records and liens

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Claims history for 7 years.** CLUE (Comprehensive Loss Underwriting Exchange) tracks personal insurance claims history for both homeowners and auto, following both the person and the property.
</details>

### Question 16 — Rating
A combined ratio of 108% means the insurer is:

A. Earning an 8% underwriting profit
B. Losing 8 cents on every premium dollar from underwriting
C. 8% more efficient than the industry average
D. 108% funded

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. Losing 8 cents on every premium dollar from underwriting.** A combined ratio above 100% means underwriting losses. The insurer may still be profitable overall if investment income offsets the underwriting loss.
</details>

### Question 17 — Claims
After paying a first-party auto claim, the insurer pursues the at-fault driver's insurer to recover the payment. This is called:

A. Indemnification
B. Contribution
C. Subrogation
D. Arbitration

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Subrogation.** The insurer "steps into the shoes" of the insured to recover from the responsible party. If successful, the insured also gets their deductible back.
</details>

### Question 18 — Regulation
Insurance in the U.S. is primarily regulated at which level?

A. Federal (SEC)
B. Federal (NAIC)
C. State
D. Municipal

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. State.** The McCarran-Ferguson Act of 1945 established state primacy in insurance regulation. The NAIC is a coordinating body, not a regulator. Each state has its own Department of Insurance.
</details>

### Question 19 — Flood Insurance
The maximum residential building coverage under the NFIP is:

A. $100,000
B. $150,000
C. $250,000
D. $500,000

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. $250,000.** NFIP max is $250K for building and $100K for contents for residential properties. Higher limits require private flood insurance.
</details>

### Question 20 — CIC: Agent Scenario
A client calls to report they started renting their spare bedroom on Airbnb. Their agent should:

A. Congratulate them and take no action — the HO covers it
B. Add a scheduled personal property endorsement
C. Review the policy for coverage gaps and recommend short-term rental coverage
D. Cancel the homeowners policy immediately

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C.** Short-term rental creates significant coverage gaps — business activity exclusions may void HO liability and property coverage. The agent should explain the gaps and recommend appropriate coverage (short-term rental endorsement, separate policy, or disclosure to the insurer at minimum).
</details>

### Question 21 — CIC: Endorsements
Which endorsement addresses the most common coverage gap in homeowners insurance?

A. Earthquake
B. Water backup / sump overflow
C. Inflation guard
D. Scheduled personal property

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B. Water backup / sump overflow.** Sewer and drain backup is excluded from the base HO policy and is one of the most frequent and costly losses homeowners experience. It typically costs $30-$75/year to add — making it one of the highest-value endorsements available.
</details>

### Question 22 — CIC: E&O
An agent recommends a $1M umbrella to a client. The client declines. What should the agent do?

A. Nothing further — the client decided
B. Document the recommendation and the client's declination in writing
C. Increase the client's HO and auto limits instead without telling them
D. Cancel the client's existing policies

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**B.** Always document recommendations and declinations. If the client later suffers an uninsured liability loss and claims the agent never recommended an umbrella, written documentation is the agent's best E&O defense.
</details>

### Question 23 — CIC: Emerging Exposures
A client's recreational drone injures a neighbor. Under a standard HO-3 policy:

A. Coverage E (liability) covers the injury
B. Coverage F (medical payments) covers the injury
C. The aircraft exclusion applies — no coverage
D. The drone is covered as personal property under Coverage C

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. The aircraft exclusion applies.** Drones are classified as aircraft, and the HO policy excludes aircraft liability. A separate drone liability policy or endorsement is needed.
</details>

### Question 24 — Valuation
For a total loss on a personal auto, the PAP pays:

A. The original purchase price
B. The loan balance
C. The actual cash value minus the deductible
D. The replacement cost of a new vehicle

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C. Actual cash value minus the deductible.** ACV is the replacement cost minus depreciation. If the ACV is less than what the client owes on their loan, they need gap coverage to cover the difference.
</details>

### Question 25 — Cross-Program
A client has both CPCU and CIC knowledge. They're reviewing a homeowner's policy. Which of the following is a CIC-style observation that goes beyond typical CPCU analysis?

A. The policy is an HO-3 with open peril on the dwelling
B. The coinsurance clause requires 80% insurance to value
C. The client has no water backup endorsement, an older home with no ordinance or law coverage, and runs a side business from the garage — three gaps that need endorsements today
D. The combined ratio for this line of business is 104%

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
**C.** This reflects the CIC approach: practical, client-facing, gap-identification focused. Options A and B are CPCU-style form analysis. Option D is CPCU-style financial analysis. The CIC perspective is "what does this client need, and what endorsements close the gaps?"
</details>
