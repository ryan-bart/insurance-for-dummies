## Overview

If you're pursuing both designations, the good news: there's significant overlap in personal lines. The bad news: they approach topics differently enough that you can't just study once and ace both.

## Where CPCU Goes Deeper

CPCU 555/556 covers the **business side** of personal insurance that CIC doesn't touch:

- **Life and health insurance** — entire topic areas with no CIC equivalent
- **Underwriting mechanics** — how risks are selected, classified, and priced
- **Actuarial/rating concepts** — loss costs, combined ratios, credibility, catastrophe loading
- **Claims management** — the process, bad faith, subrogation, fraud
- **Distribution systems** — independent vs captive vs direct, compensation structures
- **Regulatory framework** — state regulation, NAIC, McCarran-Ferguson, guaranty funds, residual markets
- **Risk assessment theory** — loss exposure analysis, risk management techniques

**Bottom line**: CPCU gives you the insurer's perspective. How the machine works.

---

## Where CIC Goes Deeper

CIC Personal Lines focuses on **practical, agent-facing knowledge**:

- **Policy form details** — reading the form, understanding specific provisions, endorsements
- **Real-world client scenarios** — "your client calls and asks..." situations
- **Endorsement strategy** — which endorsements to recommend and why
- **Coverage gap identification** — the annual review process
- **Special and emerging exposures** — drones, EVs, solar, Airbnb, rideshare, home businesses
- **E&O avoidance** — how to protect yourself as an agent
- **Selling skills** — how to recommend and justify coverage to clients (especially umbrella)

**Bottom line**: CIC gives you the agent's perspective. How to serve clients and protect yourself.

## Study Strategy: If You're Doing Both

### Study Once (High Overlap)
These topics are covered well enough in both programs that studying either prepares you for both:
- Homeowners policy structure and coverages
- Personal auto policy structure and parts
- Umbrella vs excess concepts

**Recommendation**: Study the CPCU version for theory, then review the CIC version to add practical context.

### Study Separately (Different Angles)
These topics appear in both but from different perspectives:
- HO exclusions (CPCU = what they are; CIC = what agents do about them)
- PAP "who is an insured" (CPCU = the definitions; CIC = the edge case scenarios)
- Umbrella (CPCU = mechanics; CIC = how to sell and service)

**Recommendation**: Study both versions — they complement each other.

### CPCU Only (No CIC Equivalent)
- Life insurance, health/disability, Medicare
- Underwriting, rating, actuarial concepts
- Claims handling, subrogation, bad faith
- Distribution, marketing, regulation
- Risk assessment framework

### CIC Only (No CPCU Equivalent)
- Detailed endorsement strategies
- Special exposures (drones, EVs, solar, Airbnb)
- E&O avoidance for agents
- Coverage review framework
- Selling techniques for umbrella and other coverages

## Recommended Study Order

### If Starting from Scratch (Both Designations)

1. **CPCU 01 — Homeowners** (build the foundation)
2. **CIC 01 — Homeowners Deep Dive** (add practical context)
3. **CPCU 02 — Personal Auto** (learn the PAP)
4. **CIC 02 — Personal Auto** (agent scenarios)
5. **CPCU 03 — Inland Marine** (fill out the property picture)
6. **CPCU 06 — Umbrella** (theory)
7. **CIC 03 — Umbrella** (practical + selling)
8. **CIC 04 — Special Exposures** (modern gaps and endorsements)
9. **CPCU 04-05 — Life, Health, Disability** (CPCU-only material)
10. **CPCU 07-12 — Risk, Underwriting, Rating, Claims, Distribution, Regulation** (CPCU-only material)
11. **Combined Practice Exam** (test everything)

### If Already CIC-Certified, Adding CPCU
Focus on CPCU 04-12 — these are all topics CIC doesn't cover. Skim CPCU 01-03, 06 for any theoretical depth that CIC didn't provide.

### If Already CPCU-Certified, Adding CIC
Focus on CIC 01 (endorsements), CIC 02 (agent scenarios), CIC 04 (emerging exposures). CIC 03 (umbrella) will be mostly review with added sales perspective.
