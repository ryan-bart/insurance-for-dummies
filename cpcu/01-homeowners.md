## Why This Matters

Homeowners insurance is the backbone of personal lines. It bundles property and liability coverage into a single policy — and understanding how those pieces fit together is fundamental to everything else in personal lines.

---

## The ISO Homeowners Program

The Insurance Services Office (ISO) publishes standardized homeowners forms used (with modifications) by most insurers in the U.S. There are several forms, each designed for different situations:

| Form | Name | Who It's For |
|------|------|-------------|
| HO-2 | Broad Form | Homeowners wanting named-peril coverage |
| HO-3 | Special Form | **Most common.** Open-peril on dwelling, named-peril on personal property |
| HO-4 | Contents Broad Form | Renters |
| HO-5 | Comprehensive Form | Open-peril on both dwelling and personal property |
| HO-6 | Unit-Owners Form | Condo owners |
| HO-8 | Modified Coverage Form | Older homes where replacement cost exceeds market value |

### What "Open Peril" vs "Named Peril" Means

- **Named peril**: The policy lists specific causes of loss that are covered (fire, lightning, windstorm, etc.). If the cause isn't on the list, it's not covered.
- **Open peril** (sometimes called "all risk"): Everything is covered *unless* the policy specifically excludes it. This is broader coverage.

**Key exam concept**: HO-3 is a hybrid — open peril on the dwelling (Coverage A & B), named peril on personal property (Coverage C).

## The Six Coverage Parts

Every homeowners policy has these sections:

### Coverage A — Dwelling

Covers the home itself, including attached structures (garage, deck) and building materials/supplies on or next to the premises.

**Key points:**
- This is the primary coverage amount and anchors the whole policy
- Does NOT include land value
- Replacement cost is the standard valuation (not market value)

### Coverage B — Other Structures

Covers detached structures: shed, detached garage, fence, pool.

**Key points:**
- Default limit: **10% of Coverage A**
- Must be on the same premises (or sometimes the same block)
- Structures used for business or rented to non-tenants may be excluded

### Coverage C — Personal Property

Covers your stuff — furniture, clothing, electronics, etc. — anywhere in the world.

**Key points:**
- Default limit: **50% of Coverage A** (some states vary)
- Sub-limits apply to specific categories:
  - Cash/bank notes: $200
  - Securities, manuscripts: $1,500
  - Watercraft and trailers: $1,500
  - Jewelry, watches, furs: $1,500
  - Firearms: $2,500
  - Silverware/goldware: $2,500
  - Business property on premises: $2,500
  - Business property off premises: $500
- To get higher limits on these categories, you need a **scheduled personal property endorsement** (aka a floater/rider)

### Coverage D — Loss of Use

Pays additional living expenses (ALE) if you can't live in your home due to a covered loss.

**Key points:**
- Covers the difference between your normal expenses and what you're actually spending (hotel, restaurants, etc.)
- Default limit: **30% of Coverage A**
- Also covers "fair rental value" if part of your home is rented to others
- Includes "prohibited use" — when a civil authority bars you from your home due to a nearby covered peril

### Coverage E — Personal Liability

Covers bodily injury or property damage claims against you, anywhere in the world.

**Key points:**
- Standard limit: **$100,000** (most people should carry more)
- Covers legal defense costs **in addition to** the policy limit
- Applies to the insured and resident family members
- Does NOT cover intentional acts, business activities, or motor vehicle liability

### Coverage F — Medical Payments to Others

Pays medical expenses for people injured on your property (or by you/your family off premises), regardless of fault.

**Key points:**
- Standard limit: **$1,000 per person**
- This is NOT health insurance for your family — it's for *others*
- It's a goodwill coverage designed to settle small claims without litigation
- No-fault: pays regardless of whether you were negligent

## Key Exclusions (The Things People Forget)

These are the exclusions that come up most on exams and in practice:

| Exclusion | Why It Exists | How to Fill the Gap |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Flood** | Catastrophic, requires special rating | NFIP or private flood policy |
| **Earthquake** | Catastrophic, requires special rating | Earthquake endorsement or separate policy |
| **Ordinance or law** | Costs to bring building up to current code | Ordinance or law endorsement |
| **Business pursuits** | Different risk profile | Business owners policy (BOP) or endorsement |
| **Motor vehicles** | Covered under auto policy | Personal auto policy |
| **Intentional loss** | Moral hazard | No coverage available |
| **Neglect** | Insured must protect property after loss | N/A — duty to mitigate |
| **War** | Uninsurable | N/A |
| **Mold** (often limited) | High severity, hard to rate | Mold endorsement where available |

## Conditions You Need to Know

### Duties After a Loss
The insured must:
1. Give prompt notice to the insurer
2. Protect the property from further damage
3. Cooperate with the investigation
4. Prepare an inventory of damaged property
5. Submit a sworn proof of loss if requested (within 60 days)

### Mortgage Clause
The mortgage company is a separate insured with independent rights:
- Gets paid first from any property claim
- Their coverage isn't voided by the homeowner's actions
- They must be notified of cancellation (usually 30 days)

### Insurance to Value / Coinsurance
Most HO policies require you to insure to at least **80% of replacement cost** to receive full replacement cost benefits. If you're underinsured:

> **Penalty formula**: (Amount carried / Amount required) x Loss = Payment

Example: Home's replacement cost is $400,000. You insure for $240,000 (75% — below the 80% threshold). You have a $100,000 loss.

> $240,000 / $320,000 (80% of $400K) = 0.75
> 0.75 x $100,000 = **$75,000 payment**

You eat the other $25,000.

## Common Endorsements

| Endorsement | What It Does |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Scheduled Personal Property** | Raises sub-limits for specific items (jewelry, art, etc.) |
| **Water Backup / Sump Overflow** | Covers sewer/drain backup (excluded in base policy) |
| **Earthquake** | Adds earthquake coverage |
| **Home Business** | Extends coverage for business property/liability |
| **Identity Fraud** | Covers expenses from identity theft |
| **Inflation Guard** | Automatically increases Coverage A over time |
| **Replacement Cost on Contents** | Pays replacement cost instead of ACV for personal property |

## Practice Questions

**1.** Maria's HO-3 policy has $300,000 on Coverage A. What is her default Coverage C limit?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
$150,000 (50% of Coverage A)
</details>

**2.** A tree from your neighbor's yard falls and damages your fence during a windstorm. Which coverage responds?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Coverage B (Other Structures). The fence is a detached structure on your premises. Windstorm is a covered peril. Your policy pays regardless of where the tree came from — you'd look to your own policy, not your neighbor's.
</details>

**3.** You insure your home for $200,000 but the replacement cost is $300,000. You have a $50,000 kitchen fire. How much does the insurer pay?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
$200,000 / $240,000 (80% of $300K) = 0.833. Multiply by $50,000 = $41,667. You're penalized for being underinsured.
</details>

**4.** Your friend slips on your icy sidewalk and breaks her wrist. Medical bills are $800. Which coverage applies first?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Coverage F (Medical Payments to Others). It's under the $1,000 per person limit and pays regardless of fault — no need to establish negligence.
</details>

**5.** A homeowner runs a daycare out of their home. A child is injured. Does Coverage E respond?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Likely not. Business pursuits are excluded under the standard HO policy. The homeowner would need a separate business liability policy or a home business endorsement.
</details>
