# CIC Personal Lines: Special Exposures & Endorsements

[Back to README](../README.md) | [Previous: Umbrella & Excess](03-umbrella-excess.md)

## Why This Matters

Standard homeowners and auto policies were designed for a simpler world. Today's clients run businesses from home, rent spare rooms on Airbnb, own drones, charge electric vehicles, and have solar panels on their roofs. This module covers the gaps that standard policies don't address — and the endorsements and strategies that fill them.

---

## Business Exposures from the Home

### Home-Based Businesses

The HO-3 severely limits business coverage:
- **Business property on premises**: $2,500
- **Business property off premises**: $500
- **Business liability**: Excluded under Coverage E

**Common home businesses that create gaps:**
- Consulting, freelancing, coaching
- E-commerce (inventory stored at home)
- Photography, videography
- Tutoring, music lessons
- Crafts/handmade goods (Etsy sellers)

**Solutions (in order of increasing protection):**

| Solution | What It Does | Best For |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Home business endorsement** | Increases property limits ($10K–$20K) and adds liability ($300K–$1M) | Low-risk businesses, no employees, no foot traffic |
| **In-home business policy** | Standalone policy with broader property and liability | Moderate businesses with some inventory/equipment |
| **Business owners policy (BOP)** | Full commercial coverage | Serious businesses, employees, significant revenue |

### Home Daycare

A particularly high-risk exposure:
- Standard HO excludes business liability
- **Home daycare endorsement** adds liability coverage for the daycare operation
- Some states require specific daycare liability insurance
- Consider: abuse/molestation coverage, professional liability

### Airbnb / Short-Term Rentals

Renting your home (or part of it) on Airbnb, Vrbo, etc. creates multiple gaps:

| Gap | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **Property damage by guests** | HO covers your stuff, but rental activity may void coverage |
| **Liability to guests** | Business activity exclusion in the HO |
| **Loss of rental income** | Not covered under standard Loss of Use |
| **Platform coverage limits** | Airbnb's Host Protection covers some liability but has gaps |

**Solutions:**
- **Short-term rental endorsement** (some insurers now offer this)
- **Landlord/dwelling fire policy** (if renting the whole property)
- **Commercial coverage** (if renting frequently)
- **DO NOT rely solely on platform coverage** — it has exclusions and is secondary

## Recreational Vehicles and Watercraft Gaps

### What the HO Policy Covers (and Doesn't)

| Item | HO Liability | HO Property |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Boat <26 ft, low HP | Yes | $1,500 sub-limit |
| Boat 26+ ft | No | No |
| Jet ski / PWC | No | No |
| ATV / UTV | Limited (off-road, on premises) | No |
| Snowmobile | Limited | No |
| Golf cart (on course) | Sometimes | No |
| Motorcycle | No | No |
| E-bike (Class 1-2) | Usually yes | Usually yes |
| E-bike (Class 3 / high-speed) | Maybe not | Maybe not |

**Agent action**: Ask clients about recreational vehicles at every renewal. A single question — "Have you acquired any boats, ATVs, motorcycles, or other recreational vehicles?" — can prevent major E&O exposure.

## High-Value Homes and Collections

### High-Value Home Programs

Standard HO policies may not adequately cover luxury homes. High-value home programs (Chubb, PURE, AIG Private Client, Cincinnati) offer:

- **Guaranteed replacement cost**: Pays to rebuild regardless of cost (no coinsurance penalty)
- **Extended replacement cost**: 125%–150% of Coverage A
- **Cash settlement option**: Take the cash instead of rebuilding
- **Higher liability limits**: $1M+ built in
- **Broader water damage coverage**: Including sewer backup
- **Equipment breakdown included**
- **Worldwide personal property coverage** on open-peril basis

### Collections

| Collection Type | Coverage Needed |
|----------------|----------------|
| Fine art | Scheduled with agreed value; consider fine arts floater |
| Wine | Specialized wine collection coverage (temperature failure, breakage) |
| Jewelry | Scheduled personal property endorsement |
| Firearms | Scheduled; check for theft coverage specifics |
| Classic/collector cars | Agreed value auto policy (not standard PAP) |
| Stamps/coins | Scheduled with current appraisals |
| Memorabilia/sports cards | Scheduled with appraisals; collectibles coverage |

**Key**: Appraisals must be current (within 2-3 years). Outdated appraisals = underinsurance.

## Identity Theft Coverage

What it covers:
- **Expense reimbursement**: Lost wages, legal fees, phone/mail costs, notary fees from resolving identity theft
- **Case management**: Some policies include a specialist who helps resolve the issue
- Does NOT cover the stolen money itself (that's a bank/credit card issue)

Usually available as an endorsement for $25–$50/year with $15,000–$25,000 limits.

## Cyber Liability for Individuals

An emerging exposure. Personal cyber coverage can include:
- **Cyber extortion** (ransomware)
- **Online fraud** (social engineering scams)
- **Cyberbullying** (legal defense if your child is accused)
- **Data breach** (if you inadvertently expose others' personal information)
- **Media liability** (online posts that create liability)

Some homeowners carriers now offer personal cyber endorsements. Standalone personal cyber policies also exist.

## Emerging Exposures

### Drones (Unmanned Aircraft Systems)

- Standard HO excludes **aircraft liability**
- A recreational drone that injures someone or damages property = no coverage
- **Solutions**: Drone liability endorsement, standalone drone insurance
- FAA registration required for drones over 0.55 lbs
- Commercial drone use requires a Part 107 license AND commercial coverage

### Electric Vehicles

- Covered under standard PAP like any other car
- **Unique considerations**:
  - Higher repair costs (battery, sensors, specialized body shops)
  - Battery replacement can total a vehicle that looks fine externally
  - Home charging equipment (EVSE) — is it covered under the HO?
    - Generally yes as part of the dwelling (Coverage A) if hardwired
    - Portable chargers under Coverage C (personal property)
  - **EV battery fires** — covered under HO if they damage the home, under PAP if they damage the car

### Solar Panels

- **Owned panels**: Part of the dwelling — covered under Coverage A
- **Leased panels**: NOT your property — the leasing company insures them, but verify
- **Coverage A adequacy**: Solar panels add $15K–$30K+ to replacement cost. Increase Coverage A.
- **Liability**: If panels detach in a storm and injure someone, Coverage E responds
- **Lost energy income**: If panels are damaged and you lose net metering credits, this is NOT covered under a standard HO

### Home EV Chargers

- Level 2 (240V hardwired): Part of the dwelling, covered under Coverage A
- Damage from electrical surge: May need equipment breakdown endorsement
- Liability from a guest using your charger and getting injured: Coverage E should respond

## The Coverage Review Framework

A systematic approach to identifying gaps at every renewal:

### The 10-Question Annual Review

1. **Any changes to your home?** (Renovations, additions, new roof)
2. **Any new high-value items?** (Jewelry, art, electronics, collections)
3. **Any new vehicles or recreational equipment?** (Cars, boats, ATVs, drones)
4. **Do you work from home at all?** (Even part-time, side hustle)
5. **Are you renting any part of your property?** (Airbnb, long-term rental, garage)
6. **Any new household members or changes?** (New drivers, roommates, aging parents)
7. **Any new pets?** (Especially dogs — breed questions)
8. **Have you started any delivery/rideshare driving?** (Uber, DoorDash)
9. **Any solar panels, EV chargers, or smart home upgrades?** (Coverage A adequacy)
10. **Have you reviewed your liability limits and umbrella recently?** (Always end here)

### Document Everything

- **Recommendations made**: Write them down
- **Recommendations declined**: Get it in writing (coverage rejection form)
- **Why it matters**: If a client has an uninsured loss and you never brought it up, that's E&O exposure. If you recommended it and they declined in writing, you're protected.

## Practice Questions

**1.** Your client sells handmade candles on Etsy and stores $8,000 in inventory in their garage. Their HO-3 has standard limits. How much of that inventory is covered?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
$2,500 — the business personal property on-premises sub-limit. The remaining $5,500 is uninsured. They need at minimum a home business endorsement, or potentially a separate business policy if the operation is growing.
</details>

**2.** A client lists their guest bedroom on Airbnb. They don't mention it to their agent. A guest is injured and sues. Does the HO respond?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Likely not. Short-term rental is a business activity, and business liability is excluded from the standard HO. The insurer may also argue that the rental activity voided the policy entirely. The client should have disclosed this activity and obtained appropriate coverage (short-term rental endorsement or separate policy). Airbnb's Host Protection offers some backup, but it has limitations and is secondary.
</details>

**3.** A client adds $25,000 in solar panels to their roof but doesn't update their homeowners policy. A hailstorm destroys the panels. What's the issue?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Coverage A may be insufficient. If the replacement cost of the home (including the panels) now exceeds the Coverage A limit, the client may face a coinsurance penalty. The panels are part of the dwelling and should have been reflected in an increase to Coverage A.
</details>

**4.** Your client's 14-year-old flies a recreational drone into a neighbor's window, breaking it and injuring the neighbor. Does the HO cover this?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Probably not. Standard HO policies exclude aircraft liability, and drones are classified as aircraft. The property damage and bodily injury from the drone incident would not be covered. The client needs a drone liability endorsement or separate drone insurance.
</details>

**5.** Why should agents document coverage recommendations that clients decline?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
E&O protection. If a client suffers an uninsured loss and claims the agent never recommended adequate coverage, written documentation of the recommendation and the client's declination is the agent's best defense. Without documentation, it becomes a "he said, she said" situation that often goes against the agent.
</details>
